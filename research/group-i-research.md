**Manager:** Didier Deschamps (since 2012; confirmed this will be his final tournament)
**FIFA Ranking:** 1st

### NORWAY

**Manager:** Stale Solbakken
**FIFA Ranking:** 31st
**Formation:** 4-3-3 (fluid; can shift to 4-2-3-1 or pressing diamond)

Uses possession as protection ("defending with the ball"). Devastating on the counter through Haaland, with Odegaard as the "tactical quarterback" triggering the press. First WC since 1998.

### IRAQ

**Manager:** Graham Arnold (Australian; appointed May 2025. Previously coached Australia to R16 at WC 2022. First Australian to take two nations to a WC.)
**FIFA Ranking:** ~57th
**Formation:** Not publicly detailed; Arnold is described as a tactician who "starts moving the salt and peppers to talk about tactics." Likely a pragmatic setup with defensive discipline and counter-attacking bite.
**Tactical style:** Defensive resilience + counter-attacking. Man management is Arnold's "greatest strength." Created strong team culture despite language barriers (uses interpreter), limited preparation time, and wartime logistics. Banned social media during playoff camp to maintain focus.

#### Qualifying Record (AFC -- all rounds)
Total: 21 matches across 5+ rounds (most of any team globally)

**Second Round (Group F):** P6 W6 D0 L0 | GF 17 GA 2
- Vietnam 0-1 Iraq, Iraq 1-0 Philippines, Philippines 0-5 Iraq, Indonesia 0-2 Iraq, Iraq 3-1 Vietnam, Iraq 5-1 Indonesia

**Third Round (Group B):** Mixed results vs Australia, South Korea, Jordan, Palestine, Oman, Kuwait. Included losses to Palestine (1-2) and South Korea (0-2).

**Fourth Round (Centralized):** Iraq 1-0 Indonesia, Saudi Arabia 0-0 Iraq. Advanced over UAE.

**Fifth Round (Playoff vs UAE):**
- Leg 1: UAE 1-1 Iraq
- Leg 2: Iraq 2-1 UAE (Amir Al-Ammari stoppage-time penalty). Won 3-2 on aggregate.

**Inter-Confederation Playoff (Monterrey, Mexico):**
- Iraq 2-1 Bolivia (Apr 1, 2026). Al-Hamadi 10', Hussein 53'. 48th and final qualifier.

- Arnold: "99% of my players have never been on the same field as these guys [France, Norway, Senegal]. I call it the group of excitement."

### Key Matchday Dynamics

**MD1 (Jun 16): France vs Senegal**
- 2002 WC rematch. Senegal upset France then -- can they do it again?
- Senegal have nothing to lose; Mane's last WC dance. France may be cautious in opener.
- Venue: MetLife Stadium (WC final venue). France familiar from friendlies in March.

**MD1 (Jun 16): Iraq vs Norway**
- Iraq's first WC match in 40 years. Enormous emotional occasion.
- Norway with Haaland but possibly without Odegaard.
- Both teams' first-ever meeting.

**MD2 (Jun 22): France vs Iraq / Norway vs Senegal**
- France should handle Iraq. Norway vs Senegal is the group's decisive swing match.
- If Senegal beat France on MD1, this match determines who joins France in R32.

**MD3 (Jun 26): Norway vs France / Senegal vs Iraq**
- If France have already qualified, they may rotate. Norway's chance for a result.
- Senegal should beat Iraq in Toronto to secure 3rd-place cushion at minimum.
- Both MD3 matches simultaneous -- scenario-dependent permutations likely.

### Key Questions
1. Will Odegaard be fit? Norway without him is a fundamentally different (worse) team.
2. Can Haaland break his late-season scoring drought in time?
3. Will Senegal's Saudi-based players (Mane, Koulibaly, Mendy) have the physical sharpness for a North American summer tournament?
4. How will Iraq handle the step up in quality after their grueling 21-match qualifying slog?
5. Can France avoid the 2002 scenario -- complacency in the opener vs Senegal?
