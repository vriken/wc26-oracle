## 1. ARGENTINA

### Manager & Tactics
- **Manager:** Lionel Scaloni (appointed August 2018)
- **Formation:** 4-3-3 / 4-4-2 hybrid (also uses 3-5-2, 4-2-3-1)
- **Style:** Fluid system combining traditional Argentine "La Nuestra" with modern pressing. High-intensity pressing, compact defensive lines, rapid transitions. Enzo Fernandez operates as playmaker at the base of midfield. Full-backs (especially Molina) are critical to attacking width. De Paul provides grit and work rate. System designed to platform Messi while not being wholly dependent on him.
- **Strengths:** Tournament experience (won 2022 WC, 2021 & 2024 Copa America), elite depth at every position, world-class GK in Emi Martinez, institutional winning mentality
- **Weaknesses:** Aging core (Messi 38, Otamendi 38, Di Maria retired), managing Messi's minutes, injury concerns in defense

## 2. ALGERIA

### Manager & Tactics
- **Manager:** Vladimir Petkovic (appointed 2024; previously managed Switzerland at Euro 2020 QF and 2018 WC R16)
- **Formation:** 4-3-3 / 3-4-2-1 (flexible depending on opponent)
- **Style:** Intense, attack-minded football built around combination play of Mahrez, Amoura, Gouiri, and Aouar. Can shift to a 3-4-2-1 against stronger opponents (used vs Uruguay in 0-0 draw). Strong set-piece delivery via Mahrez.
- **Strengths:** Individual attacking quality (Amoura, Mahrez, Gouiri), AFCON experience, strong qualifying campaign, Petkovic's tournament pedigree
- **Weaknesses:** 12-year World Cup absence (last in 2014), inconsistency against top-level opposition, defensive vulnerability

## 3. AUSTRIA

### Manager & Tactics
- **Manager:** Ralf Rangnick (appointed April 2024; former Man Utd interim, architect of Red Bull pressing philosophy)
- **Formation:** 4-2-3-1 (primary); can shift to 4-4-2
- **Style:** Gegenpressing. High-intensity pressing, compact distances, quick transitions. Sabitzer operates as creative #10 hub. Seiwald-Schlager double pivot provides structural balance. Arnautovic leads the line with physicality and experience. Rangnick demands high work rate from every player.
- **Strengths:** Tactical discipline, pressing intensity, strong set-piece delivery (Sabitzer), tournament-tested (progressed past group in last 2 Euros), Rangnick's tactical rigor
- **Weaknesses:** Lack of World Cup experience (first since 1998), limited individual star quality vs top nations, depth behind starters

## 4. JORDAN

### Manager & Tactics
- **Manager:** Jamal Sellami (appointed June 2024; Moroccan; played 38 caps for Morocco including 1998 WC; played for Besiktas)
- **Formation:** 4-5-1 / 3-4-3 (flexible)
- **Style:** Defensive discipline combined with quick counter-attacking. Compact defensive block, rapid transitions, wing play through Al-Taamari. Pragmatic approach -- will defend deep and hit on the break. Well-organized and difficult to break down.
- **Strengths:** Tactical resilience (unbeaten in 15 of Sellami's 21 games), counter-attacking quality through Al-Taamari, strong team spirit, nothing to lose as debutants
- **Weaknesses:** Limited depth, no World Cup experience, few players at top European clubs, poor recent friendly form

### Key Battleground: Algeria vs Austria (Jun 27, Kansas City)
This is effectively the decider for 2nd place. Experts describe it as "a coin flip." Algeria's attacking firepower (Amoura, Gouiri, Mahrez) is more individually gifted than Austria's front line, but Rangnick's tactical system and Austria's defensive discipline give them an edge in organization. Algeria's 1982 World Cup history with Austria adds emotional weight.

### Upset Alert
Multiple analysts compare Jordan to Saudi Arabia in 2022 (who beat Argentina 2-1 in the group stage). Jordan's final match vs Argentina (Jun 27, Dallas) could see a rotated Argentine side if qualification is already secured. Jordan's defensive discipline + counter-attacking through Al-Taamari could produce a historic result.

### Champion's Curse
No defending champion has won back-to-back World Cups since Brazil (1958-1962). France (2022), Germany (2018), Spain (2014), Italy (2010), and Brazil (2006) all failed to repeat. Argentina will be tested by this historical trend.

### World Cup Format (2026)
- 48 teams, 12 groups of 4
- Top 2 from each group advance (24 teams)
- 8 best third-placed teams also advance (total 32 teams in knockout round)
- This means finishing 3rd is not automatically elimination -- Algeria or Jordan could still advance with a strong 3rd-place finish
