### Tactical Style

Spain under De la Fuente have moved beyond classic tiki-taka into something faster, more vertical, and more ruthless. High possession (typically 60%+) combined with rapid transitions through the wings. Lamine Yamal and Nico Williams provide terrifying width. Rodri anchors a midfield that controls tempo. The press is intense and organized. De la Fuente favors a 4-2-3-1 that morphs into a 4-3-3 in possession.

Key tactical identity: possession dominance, quick vertical attacks, wing play through Yamal/Williams, midfield control via Rodri/Pedri.

### Tactical Style

Renard builds around an aggressive high press and offside trap. When the press works, Saudi Arabia can trouble top teams (see: Argentina 2022). When it fails, the space in behind is devastating. The defense has been the team's biggest vulnerability recently -- conceding in every Arab Cup match. Analyst Hamed Al Balawi has criticized Renard's offside trap as "ideas that have died in modern football." The team needs Al-Dawsari's creativity and Al-Buraikan's finishing to fire simultaneously.

### The Story

Cape Verde is the ultimate underdog story of the 2026 World Cup. A volcanic archipelago of ~525,000 people off the west coast of Africa, they became the smallest country by land area ever to qualify for a World Cup (until Curacao qualified 5 weeks later). They topped CAF Group D ahead of Cameroon, beat Cameroon at home, and reached the AFCON 2023 quarterfinals. Their squad is largely built from the diaspora -- players born or raised in Portugal, France, Netherlands, Ireland, and the USA.

### Tactical Style

Compact 4-3-3 or 4-2-3-1 that protects the center and attacks quickly through the wings. Defensively disciplined, physically tough, tightly connected. Under Bubista, Cape Verde prioritize shape and set-piece efficiency. They are unlikely to try to outplay Spain or Uruguay -- the game plan will be organized defense, quick transitions, and capitalizing on set pieces.

## Group Assessment

### Power Ranking

1. **Spain** (FIFA #2) -- Overwhelming favorites. Euro 2024 champions. Deepest squad in the tournament. Yamal, Pedri, Rodri trio is world-class. Only question is whether Rodri and Nico Williams are fully fit. Should win all 3 matches.

2. **Uruguay** (FIFA #16) -- Strong on paper with Valverde as the standout. But internal tensions under Bielsa, inconsistent recent form (5-1 loss to USA, many draws), and Darwin Nunez's match fitness concerns are real risks. Should still advance as 2nd but could stumble.

3. **Saudi Arabia** (FIFA #60) -- Proven giant-killers (Argentina 2022) but current form is terrible (0-4 vs Egypt, 1-2 vs Serbia in March). Renard under pressure. Aging talisman Al-Dawsari. The high-press system is being exposed. Could spring an upset on any given day but unlikely to advance.

4. **Cape Verde** (FIFA #68) -- Historic debutants. Brilliant qualifying campaign but the gap in quality to Spain/Uruguay is vast. Best chance is vs Saudi Arabia on the final matchday. A point or even a win there would be a monumental achievement. Even three points may not be enough for 3rd-place advancement if they lose heavily to Spain and Uruguay.

### Key Match: Cape Verde vs Saudi Arabia (Jun 26, Houston)

This is the match that likely determines 3rd place. Both teams need it. Saudi Arabia are favorites but Cape Verde have already shown they can beat more fancied opponents (Cameroon). A Cape Verde win would be one of the stories of the tournament.

### Key Match: Uruguay vs Spain (Jun 26, Guadalajara)

Defines who tops the group. If both have already qualified (likely), this could still matter for Round of 32 seeding and opponent.

### Wildcard Factors

- **Suarez comeback:** If Bielsa recalls the 39-year-old, it changes Uruguay's dynamic -- could inspire or could backfire
- **Rodri fitness:** If fully fit, Spain are near-unbeatable. If not, the midfield balance shifts
- **Nico Williams groin:** If he misses the WC, Spain lose their left-wing threat (though backups exist)
- **Saudi Arabia upset potential:** Renard has done it before (Argentina 2022). Never rule them out in a single match
- **Heat and humidity:** Matches in Atlanta, Miami, Houston in June -- could favor teams used to warm conditions (Saudi Arabia, Cape Verde)
