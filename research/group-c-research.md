## 1. BRAZIL

### Manager & Tactics
- **Manager:** Carlo Ancelotti (appointed January 2025)
- **Formation:** 4-3-3 / 4-2-3-1
- **Style:** More structured and defensively organized than previous Brazil cycles. Patient buildup play to release the front three into high-value attacking zones. Mid-to-high pressing block in coordinated waves. Ancelotti's Real Madrid pedigree shows -- control over spectacle. The attack still runs through individual quality (Vinicius, Raphinha), but the framework is cleaner.
- **Strengths:** Elite wide forwards, deep center-back pool, midfield prioritizes control, Ancelotti's big-game experience
- **Weaknesses:** Injury crisis thinning the squad, less than a year into the Ancelotti era (limited time to build chemistry), struggled badly in qualifying (worst CONMEBOL campaign ever), no settled No. 9

## 2. MOROCCO

### Manager & Tactics
- **Manager:** Mohamed Ouahbi (appointed late February/early March 2026, replacing Walid Regragui)
- **Formation:** 4-3-3 / 4-1-4-1 / 4-2-3-1
- **Style:** Compact and disciplined without the ball, fast and direct in transition. Morocco's defensive geometry, midfield compactness, and emotional discipline deny opponents oxygen. Dangerous wide progression via Hakimi (right) and Mazraoui (left). Brahim Diaz as the primary creator between the lines. Genuine transition threat -- equally comfortable attacking after defensive recoveries.
- **Strengths:** Elite defensive organization, tournament pedigree (2022 semifinalists), explosive transition game, Hakimi as a game-changer, squad depth across Europe's top leagues
- **Weaknesses:** Coaching change 3 months before the WC (Ouahbi's first senior role), dependence on transitions vs teams that sit deep, unclear striker hierarchy
- **Coaching change context:** Regragui resigned after losing the 2025 AFCON final. Ouahbi promoted from U-20 coach (won the 2025 U-20 World Cup). Assistants include Joao Sacramento (ex-Mourinho assistant) and Youssouf Hadji.

## 3. HAITI

### Manager & Tactics
- **Manager:** Sebastien Migne (French, appointed 2024)
- **Previous roles:** Kenya (AFCON 2019), Congo, Equatorial Guinea; Cameroon assistant at 2022 WC
- **Formation:** 4-2-3-1 / 4-4-2 (defensive)
- **Style:** Defensive compactness first. Built on moments of quality rather than sustained territorial control. Direct forward progression after winning the ball. Counter-attacking identity. Haiti's attacking game relies on transition speed and set pieces rather than positional play.
- **Strengths:** Emotional intensity, resilient mentality (qualified despite playing every match on neutral ground), disciplined defensive shape, Nazon's finishing
- **Weaknesses:** Lack of squad depth and quality compared to top-tier opponents, no players in Europe's top 5 leagues except Bellegarde (Wolves, relegated) and Delcroix, limited big-game experience at senior tournament level

## 4. SCOTLAND

### Manager & Tactics
- **Manager:** Steve Clarke (appointed 2019; longest-serving Scotland manager ever, 74+ games)
- **Formation:** 3-5-2 / 3-4-2-1 (primary), can shift to 5-4-1 (defensive), 4-1-4-1, or 4-3-2-1
- **Style:** Mourinho school (Clarke was Mourinho's assistant at Chelsea). Defend deep, stay compact, force opponents wide, pounce on turnovers. Counter-attacking with precision. Set pieces are a major weapon. Wing-backs (Robertson, Hickey) provide width. In possession against weaker teams, can dominate; against stronger sides, sits deep and counters.
- **Strengths:** Tactical discipline, set-piece threat, big-game mentality (McTominay, Robertson, McGinn), defensive organization (0.6 xGA per match in qualifying), adaptable formation
- **Weaknesses:** Limited attacking depth beyond key players, can struggle to create in open play vs technically superior opponents, goalkeeper uncertainty (Gunn barely playing at Nottingham Forest), aging core

Source: [RotoWire Group C Preview](https://www.rotowire.com/soccer/article/2026-world-cup-group-c-preview-brazil-morocco-scotland-haiti-tactics-lineups-set-pieces-odds-109040), [TotalFootballAnalysis](https://totalfootballanalysis.com/match-analysis/scotland-denmark-2026-fifa-world-cup-qualifiers-tactical-analysis-tactics), [Sky Sports](https://www.skysports.com/football/news/12017/13526075/scotland-at-world-cup-2026-what-are-the-selection-questions-steve-clarke-must-answer-ahead-of-naming-26-man-squad)

### Qualifying Record (UEFA Group C)
- **Finished:** 1st of 4 (P6 W4 D1 L1, 13 pts)

## Key Narratives & Tipping Angles

### Brazil
- **For:** Five-time champions, Ancelotti's tactical pedigree, Vinicius Jr as a game-breaker, deep squad even with injuries
- **Against:** Worst qualifying campaign ever, injury crisis (Rodrygo OUT, Raphinha/Bruno/Estevao fitness concerns), less than a year under Ancelotti, no settled striker, Neymar drama as a distraction
- **Tipping note:** Brazil are still the safest group winner pick despite everything, but they are more vulnerable than usual. A loss to Morocco on MD1 could create chaos.

### Morocco
- **For:** 2022 WC semifinalists, AFCON champions, 8th in the world, perfect qualifying record (8/8), phenomenal defensive record (2 goals conceded in 8 qualifiers), Hakimi world-class, squad depth in top European leagues, tournament experience and winning mentality
- **Against:** New coach with zero senior experience (appointed 3 months before WC), transition risk, AFCON final controversy may linger psychologically, dependent on transitions
- **Tipping note:** Morocco at +450 to win the group is widely considered excellent value. They are a genuine threat to finish above Brazil. The coaching change is the biggest wildcard.

### Scotland
- **For:** Strong qualifying campaign (topped group ahead of Denmark/Greece), McTominay in career-best form, tactically disciplined and hard to beat, set-piece threat, Robertson's experience, emotional momentum of ending 28-year WC drought
- **Against:** Lost both March friendlies 0-1, GK crisis (Gunn barely playing), aging squad, have never advanced past a WC group stage in history, key injuries (Hickey, Hanley), limited attacking quality beyond set pieces
- **Tipping note:** Scotland's best path is: beat Haiti on MD1, defend doggedly vs Morocco and Brazil, and hope for a third-place qualification. The Haiti match is absolutely crucial. If they lose that, they're essentially eliminated.

### Haiti
- **For:** Nothing to lose, diaspora-driven squad with some European experience, emotional underdog story, disciplined defensive setup, Nazon as a proven goal threat at CONCACAF level
- **Against:** Massive quality gap vs Brazil and Morocco, no real WC experience, squad value fraction of opponents, struggled vs Tunisia and Iceland in friendlies, no home advantage anywhere
- **Tipping note:** Haiti's realistic ceiling is 1-3 points. A draw vs Scotland is plausible (double chance +185 has value per bookmakers). Any result vs Brazil or Morocco would be historic. They are heavy favorites to finish last (-575).
