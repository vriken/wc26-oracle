## 1. CANADA

### Manager & Tactics
- **Manager:** Jesse Marsch (appointed May 2024; contract through July 2026)
- **Formation:** 4-4-2 / 4-2-3-1
- **Style:** "Maplepressing" -- high-energy pressing, fast vertical transitions, compact defensive shape. Built on the Red Bull school principles from Marsch's time at RB Salzburg, RB Leipzig, and Leeds United. Canada's identity is aggression without the ball and rapid attacks on ball recovery.
- **Strengths:** Tactical clarity, athletic squad, dangerous in transition
- **Weaknesses:** Breaking low blocks, finishing efficiency, vulnerability behind the press against technical opponents

Source: [FWCUMC](https://fwcumc.com/news/canada-playing-style/), [CBC](https://www.cbc.ca/sports/longform/features/jesse-marsch-project-world-cup-canada-soccer)

## 2. SWITZERLAND

### Manager & Tactics
- **Manager:** Murat Yakin (in charge since 2021)
- **Formation:** 3-4-2-1 (preferred), can shift to 4-3-3 or 4-2-3-1
- **Style:** Organized, disciplined, defensively solid with smart counter-attacking. High pressing with man-to-man marking. High defensive line. Yakin frequently drops Xhaka into the backline during build-up to create numerical advantages. Blend of defensive solidity and attacking ingenuity.
- **Strengths:** Tournament pedigree (RO16 in 3 straight WCs), tactical flexibility, experienced spine, consistency
- **Weaknesses:** Lack of a true world-class goalscorer since Shaqiri's retirement, over-reliance on Xhaka

## 3. BOSNIA & HERZEGOVINA

### Manager & Tactics
- **Manager:** Sergej Barbarez (appointed April 2024; 4-year contract)
- **Formation:** 4-4-2
- **Style:** Physical, high-energy, quick transitions. Not a rigid system -- built around individual quality and a never-give-up mentality. Pragmatic and adaptable. Barbarez: "I need a Plan A, a Plan B and a Plan C."
- **Background:** Former national team captain and Bundesliga star. Zero senior management experience before appointment. Also a former professional poker player.
- **Strengths:** Attacking depth, set-piece threat (Dzeko), underdog mentality, penalty shootout composure
- **Weaknesses:** Defensive consistency, aging talisman, lack of tournament experience

## 4. QATAR

### Manager & Tactics
- **Manager:** Julen Lopetegui (appointed May 2025)
- **Formation:** Possession-based system with positional discipline
- **Style:** Proactive, possession-focused identity. High defensive line with sweeper-keeper (Barsham). Tactical sophistication from Lopetegui's experience at Spain NT, Real Madrid, Sevilla, and West Ham. Building "club-like" chemistry from a nearly all-domestic squad.
- **Personal significance:** Lopetegui was sacked as Spain manager on the eve of the 2018 WC. This is his first WC as a coach. "It seems like life owed me a World Cup."
- **Strengths:** Team chemistry (nearly all play in Qatar Stars League together), two-time Asian Cup winners (2019, 2023), Afif's individual quality
- **Weaknesses:** Over-reliance on Akram Afif, lack of exposure to top-level competition, poor recent form, nearly all players in domestic league

Source: [FIFA](https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/articles/julen-lopetegui-qatar-interview), [Sofascore](https://www.sofascore.com/news/the-2026-world-cup-warm-up-qatar/), [FourFourTwo](https://www.fourfourtwo.com/team/qatar-world-cup-2026-squad)

## Key Narratives & Analysis

### Home advantage -- Canada
Canada plays all 3 group matches on home soil: opening at BMO Field in Toronto, then two at BC Place in Vancouver. This is a massive advantage. The team has historically performed well at home, and the crowd factor in a once-in-a-generation moment cannot be understated. The injury crisis is the biggest cloud over what should be a group they advance from.

### Switzerland -- the safe bet
The most experienced tournament team in the group. RO16 in the last three World Cups. Unbeaten in qualifying. Xhaka provides world-class leadership. The question is whether they can go further than the RO16 for the first time since 1954.

### Bosnia -- the wildcard
Riding an incredible emotional high after eliminating Italy. Dzeko at 40 is on a "last dance." The shoulder injury is the single biggest storyline in Group B. If Dzeko is fit, Bosnia have genuine quality (6 goals in 8 2.Bundesliga matches is remarkable for a 40-year-old). If he's not, they lose their captain, talisman, and all-time top scorer.

### Qatar -- steep uphill
The 2022 home WC was a disaster (0 pts, 1 goal). Recent form is 2W-3D-5L. Nearly the entire squad plays domestically. The gap between the Qatar Stars League and top European football is significant. Lopetegui is a credible manager, but it's unclear if there's enough time or talent to compete. Afif is a genuine star, but the team has shown they struggle without him.

### Likely outcome
Most bookmakers and analysts predict **Switzerland and Canada to advance**, with Switzerland winning the group. Bosnia is the live underdog -- especially if Dzeko recovers. Qatar is expected to finish last.

The format helps: the top 2 advance automatically, and 8 of the best 12 third-placed teams also go through. A third-place finish with 3+ points could be enough to advance.
