### Goals & Draws: Betting on an Outlier

The prediction of 12 goals (2.00 g/m) and only one draw (17%) is the most significant and riskiest call.

*   **Low Goals:** This figure is well below the historical World Cup average (~2.67 g/m) and the model's projection (2.60 g/m). It assumes all three defensive-minded teams (Egypt, Iran, Belgium under Garcia) execute their plans flawlessly, resulting in tight, low-event matches. This is possible, but it bets against the chaos, individual errors, and set-piece goals that typically inflate tournament totals.
*   **Low Draws:** The World Cup base rate for draws is ~25% (implying 1.5 draws per group). The model flags three matches where a 1-1 is the modal outcome. Picking only one draw is a bold stance that assumes nearly every match will have a decisive winner.

This strategy front-runs a strong narrative but sacrifices the safety of regression to the mean. If this group produces even one more draw or a single high-scoring game, the goal-difference-dependent standings could flip entirely.
