## 2. Group Total: 14 goals (2.33 g/m)

**Too low.** The model expects 16.1 goals. WC group average is ~2.67 g/m (~16 goals). You're 13% below the model and 12% below historical average.

The deficit is concentrated in two places:
- JOR-ALG: 1 goal vs 2.55 expected (**−1.55**)
- ALG-AUT: 2 goals vs 2.73 expected (−0.73)
- ARG-ALG: 2 goals vs 2.60 expected (−0.60)

The JOR-ALG pick is the primary offender. Changing it to **1-2** immediately brings the total to 16 (2.67 g/m) — exactly at WC average.

## 3. Draw Count: 1 of 6 (17%)

Acceptable. WC base rate is ~25% (1.5 draws per group), so 1 draw is one below expectation but not alarming. The draw is correctly placed in the ALG-AUT decider. 

If you want a second draw, ARG-AUT 1-1 is the natural candidate (model modal, Rangnick's system is designed to make life difficult for exactly this type of opponent). But this weakens the "Argentina dominance" narrative. I'd leave it at 1 draw — Argentina going 9/9 is aggressive but coherent.

## 4. Group Story — Plausibility Check

The standings are plausible. Argentina 9 pts, Austria and Algeria on 4 separated by GD, Jordan 0 pts. My concerns:

- **Argentina 9/9 is the maximum outcome.** Recent defending champions: 2022 Argentina lost MD1, 2018 Germany finished last, 2014 Argentina did go 3W. It's possible but represents the ceiling, not the expected case. You're betting on no hiccups at all.
- **Jordan 0 points** is harsh but realistic for a debutant with only 1 top-5-league player.
- **Algeria finishing on 2 total goals** (across 3 matches) is the weakest part. Their lambdas sum to ~3.95 expected goals. A team with Amoura (10 WCQ goals), Gouiri (5G Ligue 1), and Mahrez scoring only 2 is a significant under-prediction.

## 6. Key Risks and Blindspots

1. **Algeria's goal tally is the biggest blindspot.** 2 goals in 3 matches for a team with Amoura, Gouiri, and Mahrez is under-counting. Even if you keep the clean sheet vs Argentina, give them 2 vs Jordan.

2. **Alaba wildcard is under-explored.** If Alaba is fit, Austria's ceiling rises significantly — he could be the difference between a 1-1 and a 2-1 vs Algeria. If unfit, Austria's backline is notably weaker. The picks don't seem to account for this swing factor.

3. **Kansas City heat for ARG-ALG and ALG-AUT.** Both Algeria matches in Kansas City are at outdoor GEHA Field. June Kansas City can be 35°C+ with high humidity. Both Algeria and Argentina are heat-acclimatized; Austria is not. This could be a factor in the ALG-AUT decider that's not priced in — Austria's pressing intensity drops in heat.

4. **No consideration of Argentina tactical rest.** If Argentina beat Algeria 2-0 in MD1, Scaloni might rest Messi for the Austria match (MD2), not just MD3. This could flip ARG-AUT to a draw.

## Recommendation

**One change fixes the biggest problems:**

**JOR-ALG: 0-1 → 1-2**

This:
- Brings total to **16 goals (2.67 g/m)** — exactly at WC average
- Gives Algeria a more realistic 3 goals across the group
- Gives Jordan a more realistic 3 goals (they scored in most competitive matches)
- Keeps standings identical (Austria still 2nd on GD)
- Reflects that this is both teams' most open, least cagey match

**Optional second change:** ARG-ALG 2-0 → 2-1. This gives Algeria 4 total goals (closer to their ~3.95 expected) and brings the total to 17 (2.83 g/m). Slightly above average but justified by Argentina's attacking firepower.
