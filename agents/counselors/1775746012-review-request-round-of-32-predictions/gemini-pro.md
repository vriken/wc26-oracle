This is an excellent v1 set of predictions, but it leans too conservative and pattern-driven. You're systematically underestimating upsets, chaos, and the goalscoring ability of third-place teams fighting for their tournament lives.

My recommendations aim to inject more realism by increasing goals, adding a second major upset, and pushing another match to extra time, which better reflects historical knockout stage data.

### 1. Pattern Bias & Upset Candidates

Your analysis is correct: you are being too safe. One upset is not enough, and the four `2-0` clean sheets for heavy favorites feel like a pattern rather than bespoke predictions. Favorites get scored on, especially by quality teams playing with no pressure.

**Recommendation 1: Add a goal for the underdog in two of the `2-0` matches.**

-   **USA vs Bosnia:** Bosnia didn't fluke their way past Italy. With a fit Dzeko and the momentum of a historic playoff win, they have the quality and belief to score. The US is rightly favored at home, but a clean sheet is unlikely.
    -   **Alternative: USA 2-1 Bosnia**
-   **Belgium vs South Korea:** South Korea's attack is inconsistent, but Son Heung-min is world-class and can produce a goal from nothing. Belgium's "Golden Generation" is aging and has shown defensive frailties. They should win, but not without conceding.
    -   **Alternative: Belgium 2-1 South Korea**

**Recommendation 2: Introduce a second, major upset.**

-   **France vs Japan:** This is the prime candidate. Japan is not a typical third-place team; they have a recent history of beating top-tier nations (Germany and Spain in 2022) with their tactical discipline and lightning-fast transitions. France, while favorites, can be vulnerable to this exact style. Predicting a simple `2-1` French win ignores Japan's giant-killing DNA. An upset here would have significant bracket implications and aligns with the historical 20-25% upset rate.
    -   **Alternative: France 1-2 Japan**

### 2. ET/PEN Distribution & Goal Distribution

Your 19% ET/PEN rate is low, and the 2.06 goals/match is on the cusp of being too conservative. My recommendations address both issues simultaneously. The historical average is ~25% for ET/PEN, so a fourth match should go the distance.

**Recommendation 3: Push a tight match between two evenly-matched teams to penalties.**

-   **Spain vs Austria:** This is a fascinating tactical clash. De la Fuente's possession-based Spain against Rangnick's high-octane pressing machine. These styles could neutralize each other, leading to a tense, low-scoring draw where neither side is willing to risk it all in extra time. Austria has the quality to hold Spain.
    -   **Alternative: Spain 1-1 Austria (Spain win on penalties)**

### 3. Reviewing the 50/50 Penalty Calls

The two penalty shootouts you've selected are well-reasoned, but a second opinion can provide a different perspective on these coin-flip scenarios.

**Netherlands vs Brazil:** Your logic is sound; the Dutch are organized, and Brazil's penalty record is poor. However, even a depleted Brazil possesses more individual game-breaking talent. Ancelotti's tournament record is impeccable. I believe in a tight game, the individual magic of players like Vinícius Jr. or a moment of brilliance from an unexpected source is slightly more likely to be the difference-maker, even in a shootout.
-   **Alternative: Netherlands 1-1 Brazil (Brazil win on penalties)**

**Colombia vs Croatia:** I fully concur. This is a genuine 50/50. Colombia's rising stars versus Croatia's incredible tournament resilience. A penalty shootout is the most logical and dramatic conclusion.
-   **Recommendation: No change.**
