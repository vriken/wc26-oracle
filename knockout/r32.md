## Knockout Calibration

Historical knockout stage averages:
- Goals/match: ~2.2 (vs ~2.7 group stage)
- Extra time rate: ~25% of matches
- Upset rate: ~20-25% (lower-ranked team wins)
- Clean sheet rate: ~40%

Our R32 targets: ~1.9-2.1 g/m at 90min, 2-3 matches to ET/PEN, 1-2 upsets

## LEFT HALF

### M73: Czechia vs Canada — 2A vs 2B

**Context:**
- Runner-up vs runner-up — no group winner advantage
- Canada co-host but massive injury crisis (Davies ACL recovery, Eustaquio blood clot, Johnston surgery, Bombito broken leg)
- Czechia qualified through TWO penalty shootouts — battle-hardened in knockout pressure
- Soucek, Schick, Krejci vs David (Juventus 8G/6A)

**Goal-positive:** Both need to attack to advance. First competitive meeting.
**Goal-negative:** Knockout caution. Both defensive-minded. Canada injuries limit attacking output.

**Prediction: Czechia 1 – 0 Canada**

Czechia's playoff mentality tells. Canada's injury crisis too deep — half the starting XI unavailable. Schick header from a Soucek flick-on, Czechia's aerial dominance decisive. David isolated.

### M74: Germany vs Paraguay — 1E vs 3D

**Context:**
- Group winner vs third-place — significant quality gap
- Germany need to exorcise 2018/2022 group exit demons
- Paraguay's CONMEBOL resilience (beat Argentina 2-1, Brazil 1-0 at home) but away from altitude/heat
- Wirtz-Musiala-Havertz vs Alfaro's 4-4-2 low block

### M75: Netherlands vs Brazil — 1F vs 2C

**Context:**
- Elite heavyweight clash — 1974/2010 WC history (Dutch "Oranje revenge" narrative)
- Netherlands unbeaten 13 matches, Van Dijk, Gakpo, Reijnders
- Brazil depleted: Rodrygo ACL OUT, Vanderson OUT, Raphinha/Bruno G fitness concerns
- Ancelotti's control-first vs Koeman's vertical pressing

**Goal-positive:** Two attacking sides. Historical rivalry produces drama (2010 was 2-1 AET).
**Goal-negative:** Both elite defenses when set. Knockout caution.

**Prediction: Netherlands 1 – 1 Brazil (Netherlands win on penalties)**

Neither side can find a winner. Van Dijk vs Igor Thiago aerial battle. Gakpo equalizes after Vinícius opener. Extra time resolves nothing. Netherlands' penalty composure (Koeman's structured approach) vs Brazil's historical penalty fragility (2022 Croatia loss, 2018 Belgium).

Morocco's tournament pedigree (2022 SF) and squad depth overwhelm a Sweden side missing key pieces. Gyokeres pulls one back — he's too good to blank — but Hakimi's driving runs and set pieces give Morocco the edge. Potter can't compensate for the missing creative midfield.

### M77: France vs Japan — 1I vs 3F — Tue Jun 30, MetLife Stadium (New York/New Jersey)

**Context:**
- France FIFA #1, deepest squad in tournament. Mbappe 37G, Dembele Ballon d'Or winner
- Japan: 3rd in Group F (4pts), beat England at Wembley pre-tournament, 54 GF/3 GA in qualifying
- Japan's 2022 WC pedigree: beat Germany AND Spain in group stage (lost to Croatia R16 on pens)
- Mitoma, Kubo, Kamada transition speed vs Tchouameni-Camavinga pivot
- Moriyasu's "chameleon football" — can shift between 4-2-3-1 and 3-4-2-1 mid-game

**Goal-positive:** Japan's transition quality creates chances vs anyone. France's attacking depth.
**Goal-negative:** Deschamps' pragmatism in knockouts. Japan's compact defensive shape.

**Prediction: France 2 – 1 Japan**

Japan are a genuine threat — their 2022 WC group stage showed they can hurt anyone. Mitoma's dribbling and Kubo's movement create a goal, but France's quality gap is still decisive. Mbappe scores on the counter after Japan commit forward. Dembele adds the second. Japan pull one back late (Mitoma or Kamada) — they always score vs top teams — but France's tournament experience tells. Not the walkover Iran would have been.

**Prediction: Argentina 2 – 1 Uruguay**

Messi then produces a moment of magic in the 78th minute — perhaps his last great WC goal — to seal it. Bielsa's Uruguay go out fighting but fractured.
