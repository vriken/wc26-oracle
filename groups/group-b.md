## Team Form Deep Dive

### Canada (co-host)
- **2025 record:** W7 D3 L4 in 14 matches
- **Recent:** 0-0 Tunisia, beat Iceland, 0-0 Colombia, 1-0 Wales, 3-0 Romania, lost to Guatemala on pens (Gold Cup QF)
- **Key stat:** All 3 group matches on home soil (Toronto, Vancouver, Vancouver)
- **MASSIVE injury crisis (worse than initially reported):**
  - Davies: 3 separate muscle injuries post-ACL, 434 days without playing for Canada by Jun 1
  - Promise David: OUT for WC (confirmed)
  - Eustaquio: collision → ruptured hematoma → blood clot in leg, flew to Portugal to rehab
  - Johnston: hamstring surgery Oct 2025, hasn't played for Celtic since (8 months out)
  - Bombito: broken left leg Oct 2025, 7+ months out, no return date
  - Cornelius: also missing from March camp
  - Kone: muscular injury, severity unclear
- **6 of 11 potential starters injured.** Marsch filling with Sigur, Laryea, Pepple, Larin.
- **Manager Marsch:** "The health of our squad... It's not the greatest health situation right now"
- **WC history:** 0 goals in 1986, 0 wins in 2022 (L 0-1 Belgium, L 1-4 Croatia, L 1-2 Morocco)

### Switzerland
- **Qualifying:** W4 D2 L0, 14 GF 2 GA — dominant, unbeaten
- **Key results:** 4-0 Kosovo, 3-0 Slovenia, 2-0 Sweden (away)
- **Recent friendlies:** Lost 3-4 to Germany (competitive, scored 3), drew 0-0 Norway
- **Key stat:** RO16 in last 3 World Cups (2014, 2018, 2022). Tournament pedigree.
- **No major injuries.** Healthy squad heading into tournament.
- **Key players:** Xhaka (Sunderland, captain), Akanji (Inter Milan, 12 clean sheets), Embolo (Rennes, 7-9G 3A in Ligue 1), Ndoye (Forest), Kobel (Dortmund, GK)

### Bosnia & Herzegovina
- **Qualifying:** 2nd in UEFA group, then eliminated Ireland (pens 4-3) AND Italy (pens 4-1) in playoffs
- **Italy match:** Bastoni red card, Tabakovic equalized 79', Italy missed 2 pens. Historic result — Italy miss THIRD consecutive WC.
- **Key man:** Edin Dzeko (40, captain) — 6G 4A in 8 apps at Schalke (1.01 G/90, best in 2.Bundesliga). Shoulder injury in Italy playoff — ligament strain (not tear), no surgery needed, expects 1-6 weeks recovery. Should be fit for WC (10+ weeks to opener).
- **NEW: Nikola Katić (CB) knee injury** in Italy playoff — may miss rest of Schalke season. WC status genuinely uncertain. Bosnia's defensive spine at risk.
- **Other key players:** Demirovic (Stuttgart), Kolasinac (Atalanta), Dedic (Benfica), Bajraktarevic (PSV, 21yo)
- **WC history:** 2014 was first-ever WC (L 1-2 Argentina, L to Nigeria, W 3-1 Iran)
- **Only 2nd WC appearance ever**

### Qatar
- **Recent form:** 2W 3D 5L in last 10 — very poor
- **Qualifying path:** Scraped through AFC 4th Round (centralized mini-tournament in Qatar). Beat UAE 2-1 to qualify.
- **Lost 0-3 to Tunisia, 1-3 to Kyrgyzstan, 0-3 to Uzbekistan** in recent matches
- **March friendlies CANCELLED** (vs Serbia & Argentina) — by WC opener, will have played only 1 match in 6+ months
- **2022 WC (as hosts):** 0 pts, 1 goal scored, 7 conceded. First host to lose opener and finish with 0 pts.
- **Key players:** Akram Afif (2x AFC Player of Year, Al Sadd), Almoez Ali (60+ intl goals, Al Sadd)
- **Nearly all-domestic squad** — almost everyone plays in Qatar Stars League (primarily Al Sadd). Unique "club-like" chemistry but massive quality gap vs European football.
- **Manager:** Julen Lopetegui (sacked as Spain manager on eve of 2018 WC — redemption arc)

## Match Predictions

### Match 1: Canada vs Bosnia & Herzegovina — Jun 12, BMO Field (Toronto)

**Context:**
- Canada's home opener as co-host — massive crowd support
- Bosnia riding incredible emotional high from eliminating Italy (10 days prior)
- Dzeko shoulder injury — may not be fully fit or available
- Market: essentially a coin flip (Canada +110, Bosnia +280)
- First-ever meeting between these two nations

**Goal-positive factors:** Canada's home crowd energy, Marsch's high-press system, Bosnia's attacking quality (Dzeko, Demirovic)
**Goal-negative factors:** Opening match caution, Canada's injury crisis, Bosnia may sit deeper away from home

**Prediction: Canada 1 – 1 Bosnia & Herzegovina**

Home opener nerves + Canada's depleted squad vs Bosnia's playoff momentum. Neither team can afford to lose. A draw is the most likely outcome in this coin-flip match. Model λ: CAN 1.43, BOS 1.11 — 1-1 is the modal scoreline (12.5%).

### Match 2: Qatar vs Switzerland — Jun 13, Levi's Stadium (Santa Clara)

**Context:**
- Biggest mismatch in the group — Switzerland -334 heavy favorite
- Qatar's 2022 WC was a disaster (0 pts, 1 goal scored)
- Nearly all Qatar players in domestic league vs Switzerland's European-based squad
- Santa Clara: mild climate, outdoor stadium, no significant weather factor

**Goal-positive factors:** Switzerland's attacking quality (Embolo, Ndoye), Qatar's leaky defense (7 conceded in 3 WC games)
**Goal-negative factors:** Switzerland may manage the game conservatively after going ahead; Lopetegui's tactical discipline could limit damage

**Prediction: Qatar 0 – 3 Switzerland** *(reverted to 0-3 in v3)*

Qatar are genuinely bad: 0 pts as 2022 hosts, 2W 3D 5L recent form, nearly all-domestic squad, and cancelled March friendlies mean they'll have played 1 match in 6+ months. Switzerland scored 14 in 6 qualifiers. The quality gap is enormous — Embolo, Ndoye, and Amdouni/Okafor will feast. Lopetegui can organize but can't manufacture talent. Model λ: QAT 0.77, SUI 1.79.

### Match 3: Switzerland vs Bosnia & Herzegovina — Jun 18, SoFi Stadium (Los Angeles)

**Context:**
- SoFi has translucent roof — comfortable conditions
- Key match: if both lost/drew MD1, this becomes crucial
- Switzerland's tournament experience vs Bosnia's underdog energy
- Dzeko status: if he's fit, Bosnia are genuinely dangerous

**Goal-positive factors:** Bosnia will push for a result, opening space for Swiss counters. Dzeko's hold-up play creates chances.
**Goal-negative factors:** Yakin will set up pragmatically. Bosnia's defense isn't watertight.

**Prediction: Switzerland 2 – 1 Bosnia & Herzegovina**

Swiss class tells — Xhaka controls tempo, Embolo/Ndoye create chances. Bosnia's fighting spirit gets them a goal (Dzeko header or Demirovic on the break) but it's not enough. Model λ: SUI 1.63, BOS 1.04 — 2-1 at 9.6% (#3).

### Match 4: Canada vs Qatar — Jun 18, BC Place (Vancouver, indoor)

**Context:**
- BC Place is indoor/domed — no weather factor
- Canada at home vs weakest team in the group
- Canada likely need a win after drawing/losing MD1
- Qatar's 2022 WC record suggests they struggle badly at this level

**Goal-positive factors:** Canada's home crowd, David's quality, mismatch in squad quality
**Goal-negative factors:** Qatar's "club chemistry" from Al Sadd core may keep it competitive early

**Prediction: Canada 2 – 1 Qatar** *(changed from 2-0 in v2)*

Canada's quality + home support is decisive, but Afif (12G 11A at Al Sadd, 2x AFC Player of Year) is too good to be blanked across the entire group. Canada's depleted defense (Bombito broken leg, Johnston surgery, Cornelius missing) gives Afif space on the counter. David and Buchanan score for Canada. Model λ: CAN 1.75, QAT 0.96 — 2-1 at 9.8% (#4).

### Match 5: Switzerland vs Canada — Jun 24, BC Place (Vancouver, indoor)

**Context:**
- Final matchday — potential group decider
- Canada at home but Switzerland the better team on paper
- Both may already be qualified (2 qualify + best 3rds)
- Switzerland have beaten Canada in most previous meetings

**Goal-positive factors:** High stakes if group still undecided. Canada's home crowd.
**Goal-negative factors:** If both teams are through, incentive to manage the game. Tactical, controlled affair.

**Prediction: Switzerland 1 – 1 Canada**

Both teams may be content with a draw if it secures advancement. Xhaka and David trade goals. A point each suits the group dynamic. Model λ: SUI 1.57, CAN 1.22 — 1-1 is modal at 11.8%.

### Match 6: Bosnia & Herzegovina vs Qatar — Jun 24, Lumen Field (Seattle)

**Context:**
- Simultaneous with SUI-CAN — Bosnia may need a win to advance as 3rd
- Qatar likely eliminated already
- Seattle: mild climate, outdoor, no significant weather factor
- Bosnia's only prior meeting with Qatar was a 2-0 win (2016)

**Goal-positive factors:** Bosnia's desperation, Qatar's defensive fragility. Dzeko's potential "last dance" motivation.
**Goal-negative factors:** Qatar may park the bus. Dead rubber vibes if both are out of contention.

**Prediction: Bosnia & Herzegovina 2 – 1 Qatar**

Bosnia's fighting spirit carries them. Dzeko or Demirovic leads the charge. Qatar's Afif grabs a consolation — his quality demands at least one goal across the group. Model λ: BOS 1.70, QAT 1.14 — 2-1 at 9.6% (#3).
