## Team Form Deep Dive

### Spain
- **Recent form:** W7 D3 L0 in last 12 competitive. Unbeaten in qualifying. Thrilling Nations League run (5-4 over France, penalty wins over Netherlands and Portugal)
- **Qualifying:** 21GF/2GA in 6 matches — only conceded in the final dead rubber (2-2 vs Turkey). Oyarzabal top scorer with 6
- **Manager:** De la Fuente (since Dec 2022). Euro 2024 champions, Nations League 2023 winners. Has evolved Spain beyond tiki-taka into faster, more vertical, more ruthless football
- **CRITICAL fitness concerns:**
  - Rodri: back playing regularly since January 2026. Featured in 5+ PL starts, scored vs Nottingham Forest. Training fully. Transfer saga with Real Madrid is a distraction but not fitness issue
  - Nico Williams: returned to first-team training late March 2026 after 2 months out. Conservative treatment worked, surgery avoided. Race to regain match fitness — likely available but may not be 100%
  - Carvajal: ACL recovery, backup options in Porro and Mingueza
- **Key players:** Yamal (19G/13A all comps, FotMob 8.31, age 18 — most dangerous attacker in the group), Pedri (2G/9A, 90% pass accuracy, fully rejuvenated), Oyarzabal (Euro 2024 final winner, 6 qualifying goals)
- **Depth:** Squad value dwarfs the group. Even without Rodri/Williams, they have Zubimendi, Pino, Barrenetxea
- **Key injuries:** Samu Aghehowa confirmed OUT (ACL, Feb 2026, 20G in 32 apps before injury). Fabian Ruiz knee injury (individual training, dispute with PSG). Merino also injured
- **Concern:** 0-0 friendly vs Egypt in March 2026 — only flat note in an otherwise dominant run

### Uruguay
- **Recent form:** W4 D4 L2 in last 10. Lots of draws and low-scoring affairs. The 5-1 loss to USA (Nov 2025) was an alarming outlier
- **Qualifying:** Finished 4th in CONMEBOL — strong early (beat Brazil 2-0, beat Argentina 2-0 in Buenos Aires) but fell off badly in 2024-25 with multiple draws and losses
- **Manager:** Bielsa (since May 2023). Called himself "toxic" in press conference. "Humanly, I still haven't achieved acceptance from this group." FourFourTwo reports could be fired before WC. Trademark high-intensity pressing, but internal tensions are a real factor. Suarez publicly criticised the environment. ESPN Deportes described the team as "apathetic and inconsistent." AUF considered replacing Bielsa after the USA debacle
- **CRITICAL fitness concerns:**
  - Darwin Nunez: de-registered from Al-Hilal league squad Feb 2026. AFC CL R16 postponed due to Middle East conflict. Last competitive match: Feb 16. Essentially 1 match in 2+ months entering WC. Match fitness crisis
  - Araujo: took a 5-week mental health break (Nov-Jan), muscle issue in April but tests ruled out serious injury. Expected fit
- **Key players:** Valverde (hat-trick vs Man City in CL R16. Stoppage-time penalty equalizer vs England at Wembley 1-1. 8G/8A in 2026 calendar year — Uruguay's most important player), Ugarte (ball-winner, limited creative output at Man United)
- **Wildcard:** Suarez (39, Inter Miami) — open to return: "I will never say no to my country." Scored for Inter Miami Apr 5. Age 39. No official recall yet
- **Key concern:** Internal tensions + inconsistency. The talent is there (Valverde is world-class) but the collective is fragile

### Saudi Arabia
- **Recent form:** W5 D3 L5 in last 13. Alarming recent results: 0-4 Egypt, 1-2 Serbia (March 2026). Lost Arab Cup semi to Jordan 0-1. Conceded in every Arab Cup match
- **Qualifying:** Struggled through AFC — 7 goals in 10 third-round matches, required the fourth round to scrape through. Did not qualify directly
- **Manager:** Renard (returned Oct/Nov 2024). Under intense pressure — prominent broadcaster called for dismissal after Arab Cup. Analyst accused him of "stripping the national team of its identity." SAFF president convened emergency meeting. Survived but scrutiny is fierce
- **Key players:** Al-Dawsari (4G/5A in 13 SPL matches. 140+ career goals, big-game pedigree. Age 33, 100+ caps — still the talisman but age + 3 WC matches in North American heat is a concern), Al-Buraikan (2G/5A in 28 matches, inconsistent), Al-Juwayr (22yo, Young Player of the Year — most exciting emerging talent)
- **Tactical identity crisis:** Renard's high press + offside trap worked spectacularly vs Argentina in 2022, but analysts now call the system "ideas that have died in modern football." When the press fails, space in behind is devastating
- **Upset potential:** Never rule out Renard in a single match (Argentina 2022). But sustaining it over 3 matches against this group is a different ask

### Cape Verde
- **Recent form:** W4 D3 L1 in last 8 competitive. Mixed friendlies: 5-3 win over Finland but 4-2 loss to Chile (red card). Drew 1-1 with Egypt, 0-0 with Iran
- **Qualifying:** Topped CAF Group D ahead of Cameroon (W7 D2 L1, +8 GD). Beat Cameroon 1-0 at home (beat Onana). Away win in Angola 2-1
- **The story:** Population ~525,000, smallest WC nation by land area. First-ever World Cup appearance. Diaspora squad — ~14 players born outside Cape Verde, spread across Portugal, Netherlands, Ireland, France, USA, Turkey, Russia
- **Manager:** Bubista (since 2020). Has built a compact, disciplined, tightly connected unit. AFCON 2023 quarterfinalists
- **Key players:** Livramento (Casa Pia, 4 qualifying goals — scored the clinching goal vs Cameroon), Ryan Mendes (captain, 22 intl goals, aging), Roberto "Pico" Lopes (CB, Shamrock Rovers, born in Ireland, aerial dominance), Jamiro Monteiro (PEC Zwolle, technical midfield presence), Logan Costa (Villarreal, La Liga starting CB, 26 caps, valued €18m — upgrades defensive quality)
- **Realistic level:** Mid-tier European league players. Competitive when organized but the quality gap to Spain/Uruguay is vast. Best chance is vs Saudi Arabia on MD3

### Group Story

Spain are the story of dominance. Euro 2024 champions, Nations League winners, and now a perfect World Cup group stage — De la Fuente's generation is peaking. Yamal at 18 announces himself on the biggest stage. The only question is whether Rodri and Williams are fully fit for the knockouts.

Uruguay are the story of "enough but not convincing." Bielsa's intensity gets them through but the cracks are visible. Valverde single-handedly drags them past Saudi Arabia and Cape Verde, but the 0-2 loss to Spain on MD3 reveals the ceiling. The internal tensions that have simmered since Copa America 2024 don't explode — but they don't resolve either.

Cape Verde are the story of the tournament's heart. The smallest nation by land area, the diaspora squad, the debutants who beat Cameroon to get here. They don't win a match but the 1-1 draw with Saudi Arabia in Houston — Livramento equalizing in the 73rd minute — is one of the tournament's iconic moments. They leave with their heads high and a nation of 525,000 inspired.

Saudi Arabia are the story of a giant-killing reputation that has expired. The 2022 Argentina shock is now 4 years old. Renard's system has been exposed, the squad hasn't refreshed, Al-Dawsari is 33, and the recent results (0-4 Egypt) tell the truth. They don't embarrass themselves — the 1-1 draw with Cape Verde is respectable — but the magic is gone.
