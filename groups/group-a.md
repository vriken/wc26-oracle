## Team Form Deep Dive

### Mexico
- **Last 10:** W3 D4 L2 (9 scored, 8 conceded) — avg 1.0 goals/game
- **xG last 6 matches:** 1.08 — extremely low output
- **Key stat:** 10 clean sheets in 15 games under Aguirre
- **Injury crisis:** Malagon (GK, Achilles), Ruiz (ACL), Huerta (groin), Alvarez (captain, ankle — major doubt), Huescas (ACL, racing), Chavez (ACL, slim), Romo (hamstring)
- **Opening match:** Mexico vs South Africa at Azteca — exact rematch of the 2010 WC opener (1-1 draw)
- **Recent results:** 0-4 Colombia, 1-1 Ecuador, 0-0 Uruguay, 1-2 Paraguay, 1-0 Panama, 1-0 Bolivia, 4-0 Iceland, 0-0 Portugal, 1-1 Belgium

### South Korea
- **Qualifying:** W6 D4 L0, 20 scored, 7 conceded (2.0 g/m scored, 0.7 conceded)
- **Recent form ALARMING:** 0-5 Brazil, 0-4 Ivory Coast, 0-1 Austria — 5 consecutive games without scoring
- **Tactical shift:** Moving from 4-4-2 to 3-4-3/5-at-the-back — coach admits it's not working
- **Key man:** Son Heung-min (LAFC), likely final WC. Kim Min-jae (Bayern) anchors defense
- **Injuries:** Park Yong-woo (cruciate, out), Won Doo-jae (season-ending), Kim Ju-seong (knee, uncertain)
- **Midfield depth:** described as "super emergency"

### South Africa
- **Qualifying:** W5 D3 L2, 15 scored, 9 conceded — first WC since hosting 2010
- **AFCON 2025:** W2 L2, eliminated in R16 by Cameroon (1-2)
- **Recent:** Lost 1-2 to Panama in March friendly
- **Coach Broos says:** "Bafana Bafana still aren't ready"
- **Key players:** Ronwen Williams (GK, captain), Lyle Foster (Burnley, 10 goals in 24 caps), Teboho Mokoena, Oswin Appollis
- **Injuries:** Mvala (missed AFCON, expected back), Dortley (nearing return), Xoki (knee, season out), Foster (fitness concerns)

### Czechia
- **Qualifying:** Won group games comfortably but lost 1-5 to Croatia. Qualified through TWO penalty shootouts (vs Ireland and Denmark)
- **Recent playoff form:** Czechia 2-2 Ireland (pens 4-3), Czechia 2-2 Denmark (pens 3-1)
- **Key stat:** First WC since 2006 — 20-year absence. Nothing-to-lose mentality
- **Key players:** Soucek (West Ham), Krejci (Wolves, new captain), Schick (Leverkusen, 24 goals/50 caps), Sulc, Hlozek, Cerny
- **No major injuries reported**
- **Style:** Physically powerful, strong aerially, organized. Struggle vs deep blocks

## Match Predictions

### Match 1: Mexico vs South Africa — Jun 11, Estadio Azteca (Mexico City, 2,240m altitude)

**Context:**
- Tournament opener — historically cautious (2010 opener was 1-1, 2014 opener 3-1, 2018 opener 5-0, 2022 opener 0-2)
- Exact rematch of 2010 WC opener (1-1)
- Azteca altitude favors Mexico (acclimatized)
- Mexico's xG of 1.08 over last 6 matches — very low output
- South Africa's coach says team "isn't ready"
- Mexico heavy favorite (-185)

**Goal-positive factors:** Altitude fatigue for SA, mismatch in quality, home crowd pressure
**Goal-negative factors:** Opening match nerves, Mexico's defensive pragmatism under Aguirre, SA will park the bus

**Prediction: Mexico 1 – 0 South Africa**

Mexico controls but doesn't blow it open. SA's defensive foundation + tournament opener caution keeps it tight. Mexico's recent scoring rate (1.0 g/m) doesn't support a multi-goal win.

### Match 2: South Korea vs Czechia — Jun 11, Estadio Akron (Guadalajara, 1,566m altitude)

**Context:**
- Both teams' tournament openers
- South Korea in terrible recent form (5 games without scoring, 0-4 and 0-1 in March)
- Czechia riding emotional high from playoff qualification
- Czechia physically strong, good in the air — South Korea weaker aerially
- Guadalajara moderate altitude (1,566m)
- Historical: Czechia beat Korea 5-0 in 2001, Korea won 2-1 in 2016

**Goal-positive factors:** Both defenses have leaked goals recently (SK conceded 5 to Brazil, 4 to Ivory Coast; Czechia conceded 5 to Croatia). Czechia's attacking confidence from playoffs
**Goal-negative factors:** South Korea's total inability to score recently. Tournament opener caution

**Prediction: Czechia 1 – 0 South Korea**

SK's scoring drought continues against organized European defense. Czechia's playoff momentum carries them — Schick or Sulc grab the winner. Model λ: SK 1.02, CZE 1.38 — 0-1 is model's #2 pick at 12.4%.

### Match 3: Mexico vs South Korea — Jun 18, Estadio Akron (Guadalajara)

**Context:**
- Polymarket: Mexico 51% / South Korea 49% — essentially a coin flip
- Most recent meeting: 2-2 draw (Sep 2025)
- Mexico won both WC meetings (3-1 in 1998, 2-1 in 2018) — pattern of competitive but Mexico-dominated WC matches
- Matchday 2 — both teams know their group standing from Match 1
- If Mexico won Match 1, they have breathing room. If SK drew, this is must-win territory

**Goal-positive factors:** Historical WC meetings averaged 3.5 goals. SK desperation. Son Heung-min big-game player
**Goal-negative factors:** Mexico's pragmatism. Could be a tactical, tight affair

**Prediction: Mexico 1 – 1 South Korea** *(changed in v3)*

Mexico's injury crisis (Alvarez doubtful, GK unsettled) + Polymarket 51/49 pricing + draw base rate (~25%) all point to a split. Mexico drew 4 of their last 10. Most recent H2H was 2-2 (Sep 2025). Son's tournament adrenaline breaks the drought. Model λ: MEX 1.53, SK 1.02.

### Match 4: Czechia vs South Africa — Jun 18, Mercedes-Benz Stadium (Atlanta, climate-controlled)

**Context:**
- First-ever meeting between these teams
- Climate-controlled stadium — no heat/altitude factor
- Czechia likely in decent position after drawing/beating SK. SA desperate after losing to Mexico
- Czechia physically dominant, South Africa relies on pace and counter-attacks

**Goal-positive factors:** SA forced to attack = space for Czech counters. Schick vs weaker defense. Open game likely
**Goal-negative factors:** Neither team is prolific. SA scored 15 goals in 10 qualifying matches (1.5/game)

**Prediction: Czechia 2 – 1 South Africa**

SA forced to attack (0 pts after MD1) → open game. Czechia's Schick + set piece threat dominates. SA pace on counter grabs a consolation. Model λ: Czechia 1.57, SA 1.05 — supports a 2-1 or 1-0.

### Match 5: Czechia vs Mexico — Jun 24, Estadio Azteca (Mexico City, 2,240m)

**Context:**
- Final matchday — stakes depend on standings
- Azteca altitude significantly disadvantages Czechia (European team, no acclimatization)
- Mexico's home fortress — hostile crowd
- Likely a decisive match for group advancement
- Only 1 prior meeting (Czech 2-1 Mexico in 2000)

**Goal-positive factors:** High-stakes match with both teams potentially needing a result
**Goal-negative factors:** Altitude will sap Czechia's energy. Mexico's defensive setup. High-stakes = cautious

**Prediction: Mexico 1 – 0 Czechia**

Mexico's home advantage + altitude = controlled, low-scoring affair. Czechia fades physically in the second half at 2,240m. A late Mexican goal seals the group.

### Match 6: South Africa vs South Korea — Jun 24, Estadio BBVA (Monterrey, 500m altitude, up to 95°F)

**Context:**
- Final matchday — both teams potentially fighting for survival or already eliminated
- Monterrey extreme heat (up to 95°F, no AC)
- Historical: 1-1 in 2010 WC, Korea 2-1 in 2016
- Heat will sap energy — more errors, more open play in final 20 mins

**Goal-positive factors:** Desperation from one or both teams. Heat-induced fatigue = defensive errors
**Goal-negative factors:** Heat also suppresses intensity. Both may be eliminated already

**Prediction: South Korea 2 – 1 South Africa**

South Korea's quality tells in a must-win scenario. Son Heung-min delivers in what could be his last WC match. Heat causes a sloppy, open game. SA grab a consolation.
