## Team Form Deep Dive

### Argentina
- **Recent form:** W9 D1 L1 in last 11. Only loss: 1-0 to Ecuador in qualifying. 5-0 Zambia, 2-1 Mauritania in March friendlies
- **Qualifying:** 1st in CONMEBOL, 9 points clear. Most goals (31) and best GD (+21) in the confederation
- **Manager:** Lionel Scaloni (since 2018). Won 2022 WC, 2021 + 2024 Copa America. The most successful Argentine coach since Menotti
- **Key players:** Messi (29G/19A MLS 2025, 900th career goal), Alvarez (17G/8A all comps Atletico), Lautaro (Serie A top scorer, fit after calf injury), Emi Martinez (72.4% save rate, 7 CS), Enzo Fernandez (Chelsea, deep playmaker)
- **Injuries:**
  - Foyth: **OUT** (ruptured Achilles, Jan 2026)
  - Carboni: **OUT** (knee ligament)
  - Lo Celso: **OUT** (torn rectus femoris, surgery required, confirmed miss WC)
  - Lautaro: **FIT** (returned from calf injury, scored twice vs Roma)
- **Messi note:** 38 years old. Still officially unconfirmed. Scaloni: 'entirely up to Messi.' ~90% likely but delaying announcement. Extended Inter Miami contract through 2028. Won back-to-back MLS MVP awards. Scored and assisted in 5-0 farewell win vs Zambia at La Bombonera

### Algeria
- **Recent form:** 5W 1D 1L in last 7. AFCON QF exit (0-2 Nigeria). 7-0 Guatemala, 0-0 Uruguay in March friendlies (0 shots on target vs Uruguay — reinforces defensive fragility vs quality)
- **Qualifying:** 1st in CAF Group G, 7 points clear. Amoura scored 10 qualifying goals. 5th WC appearance (first since 2014, 12-year gap)
- **Manager:** Vladimir Petkovic (since 2024). Previously managed Switzerland (Euro 2020 QF, 2018 WC R16). Tournament pedigree
- **Key players:** Mahrez (c, 107 caps, 35yo, Al-Ahli Saudi), Amoura (8G Bundesliga, Wolfsburg 17th), Gouiri (5G Ligue 1, Marseille), Fares Chaibi (Eintracht Frankfurt, 23yo, 2G/8A in 18 apps — breakout creative player, started vs Uruguay), Bennacer (AC Milan, defensive anchor), Ait-Nouri (Man City, PL quality)
- **Weaknesses:** Mahrez in Saudi league limits competitive edge. AFCON QF loss to Nigeria exposed defensive gaps. 12-year WC absence means no recent tournament experience at this level

### Austria
- **Recent form:** 7W 1D in last 8. Strong qualifying followed by 5-1 Ghana, 1-0 South Korea in March friendlies
- **Qualifying:** 1st in UEFA Group H. Won first 5 games. Only loss: 0-1 Romania. 10-0 San Marino. First WC since 1998 (28-year gap)
- **Manager:** Ralf Rangnick (since April 2024). Architect of Red Bull pressing philosophy. Demands high work rate. Progressed past group in last 2 Euros
- **Key players:** Sabitzer (BVB, creative #10), Arnautovic (47 NT goals, 130 caps, 37yo), Seiwald + Schlager (RB Leipzig double pivot), Chukwuemeka (FIFA switch from England, scored on debut), Posch (Bologna, versatile), Paul Wanner (PSV midfielder, FIFA switch from Germany approved Mar 9. Debuted for Austria vs Ghana (5-1). 20yo, PSV #10, former Bayern academy. Significant squad addition)
- **Alaba wildcard:** Captain's fitness is the biggest question. Missed 115 games for club/country since joining Real Madrid. Rangnick: "he gets a wildcard." When fit, transforms Austria's backline. Contract expires Jun 2026
- **Chukwuemeka:** 22yo, on loan at BVB from Chelsea. FIFA-approved association switch to Austria in March 2026. Adds significant midfield quality depth

### Jordan
- **Recent form:** Mixed. Arab Cup run impressive (5W, final, lost 3-2 to Morocco). But 1W in 6 games outside Arab Cup since qualifying. Drew Nigeria 2-2 and Costa Rica 2-2 in March
- **Qualifying:** 2nd in AFC Group B behind South Korea. Clinched with 3-0 away win over Oman (Olwan hat-trick). Previous best: 2014 play-offs (lost 5-0 agg to Uruguay). Also 2023 Asian Cup runners-up
- **Manager:** Jamal Sellami (since June 2024). Moroccan, played 38 caps for Morocco (1998 WC). Granted Jordanian citizenship by King Abdullah II. Unbeaten in 15 of 21 games
- **Key players:** Al-Taamari (3G/5A Ligue 1, Rennes — greatest Jordanian player ever, sole top-5 league player), Olwan (9 WCQ goals, 6 Arab Cup goals, Al-Sailiya Qatar), Al-Naimat (Al Arabi Qatar)
- **Realistic level:** Quality gap is enormous. Al-Taamari is the only player who'd start for any other team in this group. Ceiling is a spirited draw or shock result on counter-attack

Argentina's dominance is the headline: Messi's final World Cup delivers a perfect group stage, just as 2022 did after the Saudi shock (albeit without the shock this time). The real drama is the battle for 2nd between Austria and Algeria. Rangnick's tactical discipline and tournament experience (Euro progression twice) gives Austria the structural edge, even though Algeria's individual attacking talent is superior. The 1982 "Disgrace of Gijon" rematch on MD3 delivers a tense 1-1 draw that sends Austria through and leaves Algeria hoping the 4 points is enough for third-place qualification.

Their counter-attacking threat forces Algeria and Argentina to defend seriously.
